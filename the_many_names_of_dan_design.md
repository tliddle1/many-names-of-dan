# The Many Names of Dan

## Overview

**The Many Names of Dan** is a short comedic browser game (15–30 minutes) built around the legend of Dan Lambourne — an HR representative whose true nature is far more vast and terrible than his business card suggests.

The game is structured as a series of 7 levels. Each level begins with a dramatic, cinematic title reveal of one of Dan's many names, followed by a quick mini-game that interprets that name as literally and absurdly as possible. The tone throughout is deadly-serious epic fantasy narration applied to the most mundane HR situations imaginable.

The player starts knowing Dan only as "Dan Lambourne, HR Representative." By the final level, they have witnessed the full breadth of his legend.

---

## Structure & Flow

### Opening Crawl
The game opens with slow-scrolling gold text on black, Star Wars style:

> *In the land of cubicles and fluorescent light, there exists a man. You know him as Dan. But Dan is merely the surface — the mask worn for the comfort of mortals. Across realms seen and unseen, he is known by many names. And today... the names shall be revealed.*

### Level Progression
Each level follows the same rhythm:

1. **The Reveal** — Screen goes dark. A new title fades in with dramatic weight, like a Dark Souls boss name appearing. Musical sting. Brief flavor text.
2. **The Mini-Game** — A short, self-contained game (1–3 minutes) themed around the name.
3. **Victory Scroll** — Narrator text praising Dan's triumph in absurdly grandiose language.
4. **Transition** — *"But this is not his only name..."* — leads into the next reveal.

### Difficulty Arc
The games start dead simple and get slightly more involved, but nothing should ever be truly hard. The point is the comedy, not the challenge. If the player fails, the narrator should roast them gently before letting them retry.

## Level 1: Dan Lambourne

### The Reveal
> **DAN LAMBOURNE**
> *Human Resources Representative*
>
> *"A simple name. A simple man. Or so they would have you believe."*

### The Mini-Game: "A Day at the Desk"
**Genre:** Point-and-click / simple clicker

The screen shows Dan sitting at a desk. Emails arrive in his inbox one at a time. The player clicks each email to open it, reads a brief (funny) subject line, and clicks either "Approve" or "Deny."

**Sample emails:**
- *"REQUEST: Can I bring my emotional support iguana to the office?"* — Deny
- *"REQUEST: Permission to work from home (I am already home, I just didn't come in)"* — Deny
- *"REQUEST: Extra chair for my desk (mine has achieved sentience and left)"* — Approve
- *"REQUEST: Can we change Casual Friday to Casual Every Day?"* — Deny

There are no wrong answers. Every choice Dan makes is accompanied by a stamp sound effect and a brief one-liner reaction. After 6–8 emails, the narrator interrupts:

> *"A fine day's work for Dan Lambourne. But the realm stirs. There are those who know him by... another name."*

### Tone
Mundane. Ordinary. The calm before the storm. This level exists to establish the baseline so every level after it hits harder by contrast.

---

## Level 2: Arbiter of the 7th Circle of Human Resources

### The Reveal
> **ARBITER OF THE 7TH CIRCLE OF HUMAN RESOURCES**
>
> *"Below the breakroom. Below the basement. Below the sub-basement where they keep the 2003 holiday decorations. There lies the 7th Circle. And there, Dan sits in judgment."*

### The Mini-Game: "Judgment of the Condemned"
**Genre:** Sorting / judgment game (Papers, Please style)

Dan sits on a throne of filing cabinets in a fiery hellscape. Souls of corporate offenders float before him one at a time. Each soul has a placard describing their sin. Dan must pass judgment by dragging them to the left (FORGIVEN — they ascend in a beam of light) or to the right (CAST DEEPER — they plummet into flames).

**Sample sins:**
- *"Microwaved fish in the shared kitchen. Twice."*
- *"Replied-all to a company-wide email to say 'Thanks!'"*
- *"Took the last cup of coffee and did not make more."*
- *"Scheduled a meeting that could have been an email."*
- *"Ate someone else's clearly labeled lunch."*
- *"Put in a PTO request for the entire month of December."*
- *"Used 'per my last email' with malicious intent."*

A dramatic tension meter at the top builds as Dan judges faster. The sins escalate in pettiness. After 10–12 judgments, the level ends.

### Tone
Dante's Inferno meets corporate compliance. Fire and brimstone, but about yogurt theft.

## Level 3: Master of Gold and Crimson

### The Reveal
> **MASTER OF GOLD AND CRIMSON**
>
> *"There are two colors that bend to his will. Gold — the color of wealth, of glory, of the 'Exceeds Expectations' rating. And Crimson — the color of blood, of warnings, of the final write-up before termination."*

### The Mini-Game: "The Colors of Power"
**Genre:** Color-matching / reflex game

The screen is a grand hall draped in gold and crimson banners. Objects fly toward Dan from both sides — some gold, some crimson. The player must sort them: gold objects go up (rewards), crimson objects go down (punishments).

**Gold objects** (good things — swipe/click up):
- Gold star ⭐
- Trophy 🏆
- Thumbs up 👍
- Raise letter 💰
- Gold coin

**Crimson objects** (bad things — swipe/click down):
- Red envelope (write-up) ✉️
- Red stamp (DENIED)
- Red card 🟥
- Termination notice
- HR violation flag

The speed increases. If you mis-sort, the narrator tuts disapprovingly:
> *"Gold and crimson are not to be confused. Dan would never make such an error."*

After a target score is reached, victory.

### Tone
Regal. Heraldic. Like a medieval coat of arms ceremony, but about performance reviews.

---

## Level 4: Manipulator of Low Probability

### The Reveal
> **MANIPULATOR OF LOW PROBABILITY**
>
> *"Chance is not random. Not for Dan. He reaches into the chaos of the universe and pulls out exactly the outcome he requires. Usually involving paperwork."*

### The Mini-Game: "Defying the Odds"
**Genre:** Luck / gambling mini-game (rigged in Dan's favor)

Dan stands before a cosmic game show-style wheel. The wheel has various HR outcomes written on its segments — mostly terrible ones, with one tiny sliver of a good outcome.

The player spins the wheel. It should, by all probability, land on something awful. But every single time, through increasingly ridiculous near-misses, it lands on the perfect outcome for Dan.

**Round examples:**
- Wheel is 95% "MANDATORY SATURDAY TRAINING" and 5% "PAID DAY OFF" — lands on paid day off
- Wheel is 99% "BUDGET DENIED" and 1% "UNLIMITED BUDGET" — lands on unlimited budget
- Wheel is literally entirely "FIRED" except for one pixel-thin line of "PROMOTION" — promotion

Each near-miss should have a dramatic slow-down, a fake-out where it almost lands on disaster, then clicks over to the good outcome at the last second. The narrator is never surprised:

> *"Of course. Probability is merely a suggestion to the Manipulator."*

After 4–5 spins, victory. The comedy is in the increasing absurdity of how rigged reality is in Dan's favor.

### Tone
Cosmic. Mystical. Like Doctor Strange but he's using his powers exclusively for favorable HR outcomes.

---

## Level 5: Devourer of Maliciousness

### The Reveal
> **DEVOURER OF MALICIOUSNESS**
>
> *"Where others see conflict, Dan sees a meal. Gossip. Backstabbing. Passive-aggressive Post-it notes. He consumes them all, and grows stronger."*

### The Mini-Game: "Feed the Beast"
**Genre:** Pac-Man / eating game

Dan (represented as a giant hungry mouth or a chomping emoji) moves around a top-down maze styled like an office floor plan — cubicle walls form the maze corridors. Scattered throughout are glowing orbs of maliciousness: nasty emails, toxic complaints, passive-aggressive sticky notes, and HR violations.

Dan must devour them all. Each one he eats triggers a brief flash of text showing what he consumed:

- *🔴 "I'm not mad, I'm just disappointed" (passive-aggressive email)*
- *🔴 "Someone keeps adjusting the thermostat" (anonymous complaint)*
- *🔴 "Per my PREVIOUS email..." (escalation)*
- *🔴 "I don't have a problem with them, BUT—" (gossip)*

A maliciousness meter fills up as Dan eats. When it's full, Dan briefly powers up and becomes huge, able to plow through cubicle walls. After clearing the maze, victory.

### Tone
Hungry. Primal. Like a nature documentary about an apex predator, but the prey is workplace toxicity.

---

## Level 6: Champion of the Manifest Destiny of Westeros

### The Reveal
> **CHAMPION OF THE MANIFEST DESTINY OF WESTEROS**
>
> *"In the land of ice and fire, there is one who would unite all kingdoms — not through war, but through superior benefits enrollment and a very competitive 401(k) match."*

### The Mini-Game: "The Iron Desk"
**Genre:** Throne room decree game / choice-based

Dan sits upon the Iron Throne, but it's made of office supplies — staplers, three-hole punches, keyboard trays. Subjects from the various houses of Westeros approach and present their disputes. Dan must issue royal HR decrees.

Each dispute is presented as a fantasy conflict that is transparently a workplace issue:

- *"My liege, House Stark claims House Lannister hath stolen their provisions from the communal cold-storage."*
  (Someone ate their lunch from the fridge)
  → **Decree options:** "Install a Raven-surveillance system" / "All provisions must bear the sigil of their house"

- *"Your Grace, Ser Gregor has been sending ravens of great hostility to the Tyrells after the hour of five."*
  (After-hours aggressive Slack messages)
  → **Decree options:** "Ravens shall cease at sundown" / "Ser Gregor shall attend the Maester's conflict resolution seminar"

- *"The Night's Watch requests twelve moons of paid leave. Winter is, they claim, coming."*
  (Excessive PTO request)
  → **Decree options:** "Approved — winter IS coming" / "Three moons. The Wall can wait."

After each decree, the narrator delivers the outcome in sweeping fantasy prose. Both options are always funny. After 5–6 decrees, the realm is united under Dan's HR policy.

### Tone
Game of Thrones grandeur meeting corporate mundanity. Every line should sound like it belongs in a fantasy epic but is secretly about office politics.

## Level 7 (Final Boss): The Fist of Danbury

### The Reveal
*The screen goes fully dark. Silence. Then a deep rumble.*

> *"You have witnessed the Arbiter. The Master. The Manipulator. The Devourer. The Champion."*
>
> *"But there are those on the East Bench who speak of something else. Something older. Something that cannot be arbitrated or devoured or decreed."*
>
> *"Something that can only be...*
>
> ***felt."***

> **THE FIST OF DANBURY**

*The title slams onto the screen like a physical impact, shaking everything. It's bigger and more dramatic than any previous reveal.*

### The Mini-Game: "The Final Form"
**Genre:** Epic punch-out / button masher

This is the climax. Dan faces THE ULTIMATE HR VIOLATION — a towering, shadowy boss entity that represents all workplace evil combined. It could be a giant monster made of crumpled complaint forms, tangled red tape, and broken office chairs.

The gameplay is simple: a big dramatic button appears on screen — **"FIST"** — and the player must mash it to charge Dan's legendary punch. A power meter fills up with each click/tap.

As the meter fills, the narrator escalates:
> *"The Fist stirs..."*
> *"The Fist awakens..."*
> *"The East Bench trembles..."*
> *"ALL BENCHES TREMBLE..."*

When the meter is full, the player hits one final button — **"UNLEASH"** — and Dan delivers one apocalyptic punch that obliterates the boss in a massive explosion of confetti, gold light, and shredded paperwork.

### Tone
Every previous level's comedy was subtle or dry. This one is pure absurd hype. It should feel like the most dramatic moment in video game history, and it's a man punching a pile of HR complaints.

## Ending

After the final boss, the screen fades to black. Slow gold text:

> *"And so the maliciousness was devoured. The low probabilities were manipulated. The gold and crimson were mastered. The 7th Circle was arbitrated. Westeros received a very competitive benefits package."*
>
> *"And The Fist... rested."*
>
> *"Until Tuesday."*
>
> *"Because staff meeting announcements don't make themselves."*

**CREDITS roll** — styled like a fantasy movie credits sequence, but every role is Dan:

- Directed by Dan Lambourne
- Produced by Dan Lambourne
- Catering by Dan Lambourne
- Emotional Support provided by Dan Lambourne
- Best Boy Grip: Dan Lambourne
- Key Grip: Also Dan Lambourne
- Spiritual Advisor: Dan Lambourne
- Dan Lambourne played by Dan Lambourne

Final card:

> *"And there are many other names that have not yet been revealed..."*

## Visual & Audio Notes

- **Art style:** Simple but stylish. Emoji-based characters are totally fine and lean into the comedic tone. Each realm should have a distinct color palette and background.
- **Narration:** All narrator text appears as stylized text on screen (no voice acting needed). Font should be something ancient and dramatic — a fantasy serif or uncial style.
- **Transitions:** The name reveals are the star of the show. They should feel weighty and cinematic every single time — dramatic fade-in, maybe a screen shake, gold particle effects.
- **Sound:** If possible, a dramatic orchestral sting for each name reveal would elevate things enormously. Minimal sound otherwise — maybe some click/stamp sounds for interactions.
- **Failure state:** The player should almost never fail, but if they do, the narrator gently mocks them and lets them retry instantly. This is a comedy game, not a challenge game.
